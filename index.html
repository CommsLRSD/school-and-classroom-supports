School & Classroom Support Team

The School and Classroom Support Team (SCST) partners with schools across the Louis Riel School Division to strengthen student learning, well-being, and belonging.

SCST is a coordinated, system-level team that integrates leadership, instructional support, clinical services, Indigenous education, and specialized supports into one aligned model. Our work ensures every student — particularly those facing barriers — has equitable access to the learning environment they deserve.

Supporting Every School. Strengthening Every Classroom. Ensuring Every Student Thrives.

Our Work Is Grounded In
  • Equity and reconciliation
  • Evidence-informed practice
  • Strong, relational partnerships with school teams and communities
We Work Alongside Schools To
  • Build instructional and system capacity
  • Respond to identified student and school needs
  • Close opportunity gaps — particularly for Indigenous students and learners experiencing barriers
Why This Work Matters

Divisional data — literacy and numeracy screening, progress monitoring, attendance, and well-being indicators — continues to highlight:

  • Persistent achievement gaps across schools
  • Disproportionate impact on Indigenous students
  • Students with multiple indicators of risk

Schools with the greatest need receive the most intensive, coordinated support — and supports are continuously monitored for impact.

Our Approach

Differentiated, data-informed support to strengthen every school community.

Differentiated, Data-Informed Support

SCST collaborates with schools to analyze data, strengthen instructional practice, support professional learning, mentor staff, and build inclusive and culturally responsive environments.

Data sources used to guide our work:

  • Literacy and numeracy screening
  • Progress monitoring data
  • Attendance and well-being indicators
  • Indigenous student gap indicators
  • Classroom and school context
Tiered Support Model
Tier 1 — Universal Support All Schools

All schools receive universal support focused on building strong, inclusive, and responsive learning environments.

  • Divisional frameworks and tools
  • Professional learning
  • Mentorship and capacity building
  • Consultation upon request
Tier 2 — Targeted Support Selected Schools

Targeted, short- to medium-term support focused on specific areas of need identified through data and school context.

  • Coaching and co-planning
  • Data analysis and intervention planning
  • Joint problem-solving
  • Support for identified groups
Tier 3 — Intensive Support Priority Schools

Highest level of involvement — frequent, collaborative, and responsive to complex or urgent needs.

  • Embedded, in-school support
  • Ongoing collaboration with leadership
  • Coordinated multidisciplinary teams
  • Intensive data analysis

School-Based Support Model

Equity-focused, data-driven support tailored to each school's unique needs.

Equity Support School Model

SCST provides supports based on a combination of divisional data, school-level context, and identified student and staff needs. This responsive approach ensures resources are aligned to where they will have the greatest impact, while building long-term capacity across all schools.

SCST works in partnership with school teams to analyze data (e.g., achievement, well-being, attendance, and engagement) and co-develop plans that are strengths-based, culturally responsive, and focused on improving outcomes for all learners.

Tier 3 — Intensive Support Priority Schools

Highest level of involvement — frequent, collaborative, and responsive to complex or urgent needs.

  • Ongoing, embedded multidisciplinary support
  • Regular in-school presence to co-plan, co-teach, and model practices
  • Intensive data analysis and progress monitoring
  • Comprehensive school-based action planning
  • Coordination with external agencies and specialized services

Goal: Stabilize systems, address immediate needs, and build sustainable structures for continued growth.

Tier 2 — Targeted Support Selected Schools

Targeted, short- to medium-term support focused on specific areas of need identified through data and school context.

  • Planned coaching and collaborative inquiry cycles
  • Support implementing evidence-informed strategies
  • Joint problem-solving with school teams and leadership
  • Professional learning tailored to identified priorities
  • Check-ins to monitor progress and adjust supports

Goal: Strengthen key practices, build staff confidence and skill, and address emerging needs before they intensify.

Tier 1 — Universal Support All Schools

All schools receive universal support focused on building strong, inclusive, and responsive learning environments.

  • Access to divisional professional learning opportunities
  • Universal resources, tools, and frameworks
  • Consultation upon request
  • Support for divisional priorities (literacy, numeracy, well-being, Indigenous education)
  • Cross-school networking and collaboration

Goal: Proactive capacity building to ensure all students experience a high-quality, equitable learning environment.

How Support Is Assigned

Staff are embedded in schools based on equity-informed indicators. Schools with the greatest need receive the most intensive and coordinated support.

  • Indigenous student opportunity gaps
  • Attendance and engagement indicators
  • Literacy and numeracy data
  • Students with multiple risk factors
  • Need for sustained relational support
