Who We Are

The School and Classroom Support Team (SCST) partners with schools across the Louis Riel School Division to strengthen student learning, well-being, and belonging.

SCST is a coordinated, system-level team that integrates leadership, instructional support, clinical services, Indigenous education, and specialized supports into one aligned model.

Equity and reconciliation

Evidence-informed practice

Strong, relational partnerships with school teams and communities

Why This Work Matters

Divisional data highlights persistent achievement gaps, disproportionate impacts on Indigenous students, and students with multiple risk indicators. This data informs how SCST prioritizes support:

  • Schools with the greatest need receive more intensive support.
  • Supports are constantly monitored and adjusted based on impact.

Levels of Support

SCST uses a tiered model to differentiate levels of support across schools. This model allows for targeted allocation of time, expertise, and resources.

Universal (Tier 1)

All schools receive universal support focused on building strong, inclusive learning environments.

  • Divisional frameworks and tools
  • Professional learning
  • Mentorship and capacity building
  • Consultation upon request

Targeted (Tier 2)

Targeted, short- to medium-term support focused on specific areas of need identified through data.

  • Coaching and co-planning
  • Data analysis and intervention
  • Joint problem-solving
  • Support for identified groups

Intensive (Tier 3)

Highest level of involvement. Support is frequent, collaborative, and responsive to complex needs.

  • Embedded, in-school support
  • Ongoing collaboration with leadership
  • Coordinated multidisciplinary teams
  • Intensive data analysis
